Kerala

Mineral Resources Rare-Earth Elements Environmental Impact Assessment Mineral Found Maps
  • Kerala has limited mineral resources, but it is known for its beach sands that contain valuable heavy minerals such as monazite, ilmenite, rutile, and zircon. The state also has small reserves of limestone, clay, and granite.
  • Kerala's coastal beach sands are particularly noteworthy for their high content of rare-earth elements, especially monazite. Monazite contains thorium and various rare-earth elements, making it a valuable geological resource.
  • Geological research in Kerala also focuses on environmental impact assessments, particularly in the context of mining activities, to ensure sustainable and responsible resource extraction.

    • Kerala is situated in the southwestern part of India and is known for its diverse geological formations. It is part of the Western Ghats, a region with complex geological features, including ancient gneissic rocks, sedimentary basins, and volcanic formations. The Western Ghats are considered a biodiversity hotspot and have unique geological and ecological significance.
    • Kerala's tropical climate plays a significant role in weathering and erosion processes, which contribute to the concentration of heavy minerals, including rare-earth-bearing monazite, in coastal areas.
    • The state is susceptible to geological hazards such as landslides, especially in the hilly regions of the Western Ghats. Geological research in Kerala includes studying the causes and mitigation of these hazards.

    • The Geological Survey of India (GSI) and other research institutions conduct geological surveys and investigations in Kerala to assess its mineral resources, geological hazards, and environmental impact.
    • Kerala is located in a seismically active zone, and geological research includes monitoring and studying seismic activity to assess earthquake risks.
    • Kerala's unique geology has a direct impact on its rich biodiversity and ecosystems. Geological research in the state often intersects with ecological studies to understand the geological underpinnings of its diverse flora and fauna.
